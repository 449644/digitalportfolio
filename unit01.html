Unit 01

Unit 01 is about gaining knowledge about The Online World, online services, the range of the online services including: email, online data storage, collaborative software, search engines and blogging.
We had to gather as much information as we can from three learning aims: A ,B and C. In learning aim A covers online services, documents and communication. Learning aim B covers the internet, worldwide web, email, data exchange and data storage. And finally learning aim C covers the possible threats to data.

In this lesson we had to research about Cloud computing and the benefits and drawbacks, Ubiquitous Computing, RFID and The Internet of Objects.

In this lesson we had to take notes abouts Possible Threats to Data, Importance of Security, Social Engineering, Opportunist Threats, Computer Viruses, Other Malware and Phishing.

We learned about Search Engines and URLs in this lesson. We learned what they both are and how both search engines and URLs work. We also had a task of researching what does HTTP and HTTPS stand for and writing about it.

We learned about Packet Switching and VoIP in this session and had to take some notes. We also learned about Internet Service Provider (ISP) and Data Exchange - Transmission Modes.

In this lesson we had to write down the definition of each of these Social Media terms.

- Social Media
- Blog
- Microblogging
- Wiki
- Podcast
- Chatrooms
- Chat

We learned about The Internet and its Hardware in this lesson, we had to take notes about Server, Client, Router, Connection/Cables, Backbone, Wired Networks, Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP) & Shielded Twisted Pair (STP) and Coaxial and the pros and cons about them both.

In this lesson we learned about Internet Protocols and Connection Methods. The Key Internet Protocols are:

TCP/IP (Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol)
HTTP/HTTPS(Hypertext Transfer Protocol/Secure)
FTP (File Transfer Protocol)
SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol)
DNS (Domain Name System)

For this lesson we learned about Database Theory, Database Keys, Big Data, Structure Query Language (SQL) and Database Management Systems (DBMS)
